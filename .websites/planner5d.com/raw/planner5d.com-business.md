## Four ways to create your 3D catalogue

Running a planner means having your products as 3D models — and that shouldn't become a bottleneck. Whatever assets you start from, we have the expertise to find the best approach to get your catalogue ready.

PATH 01

You have 3D files

#### Import your existing models

Upload and test your existing files directly in Planner 5D. The self-upload tool supports a wide range of formats including .blend, .obj, .fbx, .gltf, .glb, .stl, .stp, and .usdz.

**Best for:** Models from manufacturing, ecommerce, AR, or supplier workflows.

PATH 02

Files need cleanup

#### Adjust existing assets

Our team fine-tunes your models — optimising file size, adjusting geometry, improving materials, aligning scale, cleaning textures, and preparing variants to Planner 5D specs.

**Best for:** Supplier files, CAD exports, or ecommerce 3D assets.

PATH 03

No 3D files yet

#### Build models from scratch

Planner 5D builds high-quality models from the information you have — technical drawings, dimensions, reference images, and specifications.

**Best for:** Physical products with no existing 3D asset library.

PATH 04

AI — from photos

#### Auto-generate from images

An AI tool generates realistic 3D product models from product pictures. It recognises shape and visual detail from the image, then creates a 3D model as a starting point for your catalogue.

**Best for:** Product images that need to become 3D models faster.

## Questions buyers ask before choosing a room planner or product configurator

Why not build this in-house?

An in-house build can make sense for very specific teams, but it usually means rebuilding room planning, rendering, catalogue management, AI tools, and integrations before you can validate the sales workflow.

Planner 5D vs a generic product configurator: what is different?

A generic configurator focuses on the product. Planner 5D can place configured products inside a real room, garden, gym, store, classroom, or property workflow so customers understand fit, quantity, and context.

Planner 5D vs CAD tools: when is it a better fit?

CAD tools are built for specialists. Planner 5D is better when customers, sales teams, dealers, or ecommerce visitors need a guided branded planning experience that produces commerce-ready output.

What is the Smart Wizard?

The Smart Wizard uses AI to furnish a room automatically. A customer enters the room dimensions, picks a style and how the space should function, and the planner lays out the room using products from your catalogue — ready to edit, add to cart, or send as a quote request.

What is a Design Battle?

Design Battle is a marketing offer: a themed design challenge where your customers compete to create the best space using your catalogue inside a white-label planner. It drives engagement and user-generated designs while surfacing high-intent leads and shoppable baskets.

Can it work under our brand?

Yes. Planner 5D supports white-label experiences so the planner can match your brand, catalogue, and customer journey.

Can it integrate with our catalogue, cart, CRM, or ERP?

Yes. Project data can connect to catalogue, pricing, authentication, cart, CRM, ERP, CPQ, and quote workflows depending on your implementation scope.

Do we need all 3D models before launch?

No. Many projects start with one product line, bestselling products, one room category, or a focused SKU set, then expand based on real customer behavior.

Can it support product variants and complex pricing rules?

Yes. Product variants such as colours, materials, finishes, sizes, components, and configurations can be supported, and pricing logic can be connected to your workflow.

How long does launch take?

Launch timing depends on catalogue readiness, product rule complexity, white-label requirements, and integrations. The fastest path is usually a clear launch scope with the products and workflow your team needs first.
